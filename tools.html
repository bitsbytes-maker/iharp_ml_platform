Convolution Matrix Anomaly Detection (CMAD)

GeoAI Anomaly Detection Climate Visualization

CMAD is an unsupervised anomaly detection framework designed for 2D spatio-temporal gridded datasets. It applies convolution-based matrix operations over time-series data to surface spatially coherent clusters of extreme negative events — without requiring any labeled training data.

The framework is particularly suited for polar and high-latitude datasets, where conventional anomaly detection methods struggle with irregular spatial structure and sparse observational records. Use cases include detecting cold spells, precipitation deficits, sea ice extent anomalies, and other climate extremes.

The interactive web tool lets you upload your own NetCDF or CSV data, configure detection parameters, and explore results through dynamic spatial visualizations and cluster summaries — all in the browser.
